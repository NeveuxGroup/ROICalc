Did you know? Poor communication is a known contributor to disengagement and turnover. Replacement costs for frontline roles are often estimated at ~30–40% of salary.

Our calculations are based on conservative estimates of time saved when managers move from traditional communication methods to text-first messaging.

Time savings source

Time savings come from reduced follow-ups, repeated reminders, and clarification when messages are delivered directly by text instead of email, intranet, paper, or manager relay.

Conservative estimate

Time saved is a conservative planning estimate and is user-adjustable. We default to 1 base hour saved per manager/admin per week (range: 0.5–2.0 hours), representing time recovered from reduced communication overhead. Actual results vary based on your organization's communication patterns.

Employee tier add-on

Total hours saved per manager/admin per week = Base hours + Employee tier add-on. The baseline is 500–1K employees (no add-on). Larger teams add +0.2 hours per tier above baseline: 1K–5K adds +0.2 hours per manager/admin per week, 5K+ adds +0.4 hours. This reflects additional time savings from coordinating messages across more people.

Monthly calculation

Hours saved per month = Manager/admin communicators × Total hours per manager/admin per week × 4.33 weeks per month

Total hours = Base hours + Employee tier add-on. Monthly hours are rounded to the nearest 0.25 hours (15 minutes).

Cost calculation

Manager salary saved = Hours saved per month × Manager hourly cost

This represents the manager salary value of time recovered, not additional revenue generated.
