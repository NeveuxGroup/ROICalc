Text-first messaging reaches more employees, reducing the need for follow-ups and repeated reminders.

Poor communication is a known contributor to disengagement and turnover. Replacement costs for frontline roles are often estimated at ~30–40% of salary.

Our calculations are based on conservative estimates of time saved when managers move from traditional communication methods to text-first messaging.

Time savings source

Time savings come from reduced follow-ups, repeated reminders, and clarification when messages are delivered directly by text instead of email, intranet, paper, or manager relay.

Conservative estimate

We estimate 0.5–1.5 hours saved per manager per week. This is a conservative range based on reduced communication overhead.

Monthly calculation

Hours saved per month = Managers × Hours saved per manager per week × 4.33 weeks per month

Cost calculation

Estimated labor value = Hours saved per month × Fully loaded hourly cost

This represents the labor value of time recovered, not additional revenue generated.
